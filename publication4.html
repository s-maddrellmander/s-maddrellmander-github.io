Test of Lepton Universality in Beauty-Quark Decays

Nature Physics 2022

This paper presents the results of a precise test of lepton universality in beauty-quark decays, conducted using data from the LHCb experiment at CERN. Our findings provide important constraints on potential new physics beyond the Standard Model.

Key Contributions

  • Development of advanced statistical techniques for rare decay measurements
  • Implementation of machine learning methods for improved particle reconstruction
  • Precise measurement of the ratio of branching fractions for B → K*μ+μ- and B → K*e+e- decays
  • Discussion of the implications for lepton universality and potential new physics scenarios
