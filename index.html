GNN for Molecular Prediction

As part of the competition-winning research team at the OGB Large Scale Challenge (NeurIPS), I developed GPS++, a novel Graph Neural Network model for molecular property prediction. This project showcased the power of combining message passing neural networks with transformers for improved performance on large-scale molecular datasets.

LLM Optimization

At Graphcore, I worked on pre-training and fine-tuning Large Language Models (LLMs) on distributed hardware. This project involved debugging convergence issues, optimizing for state-of-the-art throughput, and implementing efficient model, data, and tensor parallel training strategies. The work resulted in significant performance improvements for LLMs running on Graphcore's IPU hardware.

Rare Decay Measurements in Particle Physics

During my PhD at the University of Bristol and CERN, I focused on measuring extremely rare decays in particle physics. This research, published in Nature Physics, contributed to our understanding of fundamental particle interactions and tested the limits of the Standard Model. The project involved advanced statistical analysis, optimization techniques, and the development of machine learning approaches for particle track reconstruction.
